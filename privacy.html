Carioca Chile — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 25 May 2026

Carioca Chile (the “App”) is an iOS card-game app developed by Pascal Peltriaux. This policy explains what data the App handles and how. The short version: no accounts, no analytics, no ads, no trackers.

What we don't collect

  • No email address, phone number, or real name.
  • No advertising identifier (IDFA) and no third-party analytics SDKs.
  • No location data.
  • No access to your device's photo library, contacts, microphone, or camera. (If you have a Game Center profile photo, the App may display it on your own opponent's chip and share it with other players in a multiplayer room — see the “Game Center” section below.)
  • No payment information beyond what Apple handles for In-App Purchases. The Unlock-Full-Game option is a one-time, non- consumable purchase processed entirely by Apple — we never see card numbers or billing details.

What stays on your device (and optionally syncs to your other Apple devices)

The following is stored locally in the App's sandbox on your device:

  • Your gameplay statistics (wins, streaks, round times).
  • Your chosen settings (theme, language, AI difficulty, sound and haptics toggles).
  • The in-progress game state for solo (vs CPU) games, so you can resume after closing the App.

If you are signed into iCloud on your device and the “iCloud sync” toggle is on (it is by default), this data is also mirrored to Apple's iCloud Key-Value Store so it stays consistent across your other Apple devices using the same Apple ID. The data is stored in your personal iCloud account managed by Apple — Carioca's developer does not receive, see, or store it, and it is not transmitted to any third party. You can disable iCloud sync at any time from the in-game ⋯ menu; doing so leaves your existing iCloud copy in place so re-enabling later restores it.

Deleting the App from your device deletes the local copy. Resetting statistics from the in-app stats screen also clears the iCloud copy. To wipe all iCloud-stored data for the App without using the in-app controls, sign out of iCloud on your device or remove the App's iCloud data from Settings → [your name] → iCloud → Manage Account Storage.

Game Center

The App integrates with Apple Game Center to record progress against a shared leaderboard, unlock achievements, and let you see your opponents' profile photos in multiplayer. Game Center is opt-in at the iOS level: if you are not signed into Game Center, none of the features below activate.

  • Authentication. On launch the App calls Apple's standard GKLocalPlayer.authenticate API. Apple returns your Game Center display name and player ID, which the App uses to identify you in multiplayer rooms and to send achievement / score reports.
  • Profile photo. If you have a Game Center photo, the App calls GKLocalPlayer.loadPhoto to fetch it. In solo games the photo is only used inside your own device session. In multiplayer it is base64-encoded and published as part of the room data on Firebase so the other players in the room see your photo on their opponent chip. The photo is removed when the room expires (see the “What gets sent when you play online” section below).
  • Achievements + leaderboards. At game end the App calls reportAchievement and reportScore on Apple's servers so unlocks + best scores appear on Game Center. The data flow here is entirely within Apple's services and is governed by Apple's privacy policy; we do not see, store, or transmit it anywhere else.

To opt out of any of the above, sign out of Game Center on your device (Settings → Game Center → Sign Out) or remove your photo from Game Center. Removing the App also stops all further reports to Apple.

What gets sent when you play online

Online multiplayer uses Google Firebase Realtime Database. When you create or join a room, the following data is sent to Firebase and shared with the other players in that room:

  • A 6-character room code that you choose to share with the people you want to play with.
  • A display name that you enter when joining (you can use any name — we never request a legal name).
  • If you are signed into Game Center, your Game Center profile photo (see “Game Center” above).
  • An anonymous Firebase Authentication identifier that exists only to enforce per-device access rules on the Firebase database — it is not linked to your Apple ID, your Game Center identity, or any other personal information.
  • The state of the in-progress game (cards in play, scores, whose turn it is) for as long as the room is active.

This information is used solely to keep the multiplayer game in sync between the devices in the room and is deleted automatically when the room is abandoned (rooms inactive for more than 24 hours are pruned by an automated cleanup job).

Push notifications (opt-in)

If you opt in via the in-app “Multiplayer activity hints” toggle AND grant iOS push permission, the App uses Apple Push Notification Service (delivered via Google Firebase Cloud Messaging) to alert you when a public multiplayer game is looking for players. The notification only fires when the number of open public rooms transitions from zero to one or more, and never more than once per 12 hours per device.

To enable this, the App stores a device-specific Firebase Cloud Messaging token (provided by Apple via Google) alongside your anonymous Firebase Authentication identifier. The token is used solely to deliver these notifications and is automatically removed if the token becomes invalid (for instance after you uninstall the App). You can disable notifications at any time in the in-app Options screen (the single “Multiplayer activity hints” toggle that also hides the in-app activity tile) or in iOS Settings → Notifications → Carioca Chile.

Third parties

The App relies on two third-party platforms:

  • Apple Game Center for authentication, photo sharing, achievements, and leaderboards — see the “Game Center” section above. Data sent to Game Center is handled under Apple's privacy policy.
  • Google Firebase for online multiplayer rooms (Realtime Database) and opt-in push notifications (Cloud Messaging) — see the “What gets sent when you play online” and “Push notifications” sections above. Firebase's handling of the data is covered by Google's privacy documentation.

We do not use any other third-party services — no analytics, no ad networks, no crash reporting beyond Apple's built-in opt-in diagnostics.

Children's privacy

The App is rated 4+ and intended to be safe for children. We do not knowingly collect any personal information from children. The display name a child enters for multiplayer can be any nickname — we encourage children and their parents to choose a name that doesn't reveal real identity.

Your rights

Because we don't store any personal account data, there is nothing to request, correct, or delete on a server. If you want to wipe all local data, delete the App from your device. If you want a multiplayer room deleted before the 24-hour cleanup, contact us at the email below.
